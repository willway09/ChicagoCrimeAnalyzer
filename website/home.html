Crime data in the City of Chicago

Trends

Objective

The City of Chicago provides a dashboard for visualizing it’s criminal activity [1] This seems to be a valuable asset, as it enables those who view it to view crime data intuitively and interact freely in different queries. However, dashboard’s charts only provide outputs corresponding to simple queries, including the frequency of crime types, the number of crimes in a community area (analogous to a neighborhood), and the direct trend of total crime over time; it not visualize complex queries, such as monthly/quarterly/annual crime density in a specific region, whether this monthly crime rate has increased or decreased, etc. This project seeks to address those shortcomings; with an interactive website and database management system, users can get gain intuition on complex queries based on the parameters they enter.
