Query 4: Geographical concentration of Crime

Does the geographic concentration of monthly total crime in Chicago shift North/South or East/West relative to a given coordinate (lon, lat) over the course of a given time interval?

For the given (lon, lat) point (which the user inputs via a map interface), draw a vertical and horizontal line through it. For the North/South case, compute the total amount of crime which occurs above (North of) the horizontal line in a given month, then do the same for below (South of) the horizontal line; repeat for every month in the time range. For the East/West case, do the same, but instead use the vertical line, where East is to the right and West is to the left. This can then be plotted on a scatter plot (using two colors for each side of the line), and if it is observed that an increase in crime on one side of a line corresponds to a decrease in crime on the other side, then it can be posited that crime is changing itâ€™s concentration across that line. The grouping by month satisfies computation on the time axis, and the summing of crimes which pass the "side of the line" filter serves as computation for the y-axis, which makes this a complex trend query.
