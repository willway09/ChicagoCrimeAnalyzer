Query 1: Community Area Overall Crime

How has the monthly crime density (either population or geographic) in a given common area changed over a given time interval? Did crime appear to increase, decrease, or remain constant?

Crime density is defined as the total amount of crime per relevant unit. In this case, population crime density describes how much crime occurs per person (in units crimes / 100,000 people), and geographic crime density describes how much crime occurs per unit area (in units crimes/km^2). Thus, this query essentially asks how do these quantities change over time. This may superficially seem simple, but two factors complicate this query. First, because dates will be stored as timestamps, grouping crime by either month, quarter, or year requires computation. Second, population changes over time, but is only accurately recorded every 10 years in the US census. This means that interpolation will have to be employed to approximate the population when the crime was committed, which also requires non-trivial computation. Because both the time and crime density are computed, this qualifies as a trend query, and the output can be plotted in a scatter plot with crime density as a function of time, which should allow for easy visualization of trends of increase or decrease.
